OVERVIEW

Creative Guild is a recruiting service offered by Creative Mornings that we investigated during Research Methods in Interaction Design at SVA taught by Professor Jodi Leo in Summer 2018. I worked with Alex Stempel and Alexandra Weiner as "Team Impossible Burger."

My role in the team was to create the new user-flow of our micro-site proposal. Alex produced mockups and led the interview with a recruiter, and Alexandra led the synthesis of research findings to outline design goals.


RESEARCH
OBJECTIVES

bring a fresh perspective: what works or doesn’t

motivate participants to start and finish the application

help Creative Guild communicate its value for “large” companies


Creative Mornings is trying to achieve financial independence without sponsorships; a large part of their goals for Creative Guild involves attracting talent acquisition managers from large companies (defined as 30+ employees) to use their platform. According to the founder Tina, only 1.7% of users that started actually completed the onboarding process. We walked Erik, a Talent Manager at Asana, through the process again to see where pain points and dissatisfaction arose.



INTERVIEW
SNAPSHOT

Erik, Talent Partner at Asana

Erik is in charge of recruiting and posting jobs for the groups he works for and uses tools like Greenhouse to help list such jobs. He is often looking for ways to recruit high quality applicants. Thus, Creative Guild is a platform that offers a way to reach out to the community, especially design related fields. He posts other jobs there as well but expects to find creative/design professionals.

He is disappointed that his company isn’t at the top of Creative Guild and is curious as to how that can be done, perhaps through higher payment tiers. He also wishes there were metrics that could help him see how Creative Guild is contributing to his recruitment efforts.


RECURRING CONCERNS

can I talk to someone from Creative Guild?

is this for individuals or hiring manager?

who is a creative? Who is in the Creative Guild?

why are you asking for information, I’m seeking information?

how do I make my company look great?



PROPOSAL

A basic user flow can be represented by the arrival at the Creative Mornings site, our proposed intervention of the Creative Guild microsite, and if the user decides to proceed with the service: the onboarding flow


The microsite is divided into three main effective stages a recruiter might experience as they further investigate Creative Guild.



EXISTING
SITE

Arrival to Creative Mornings doesn't make clear what the site offers, but feels directed towards candidates without a clear path for recruiters.


The existing creative guild page caters to all user types, through 5 tabs: companies, individuals, jobs, pricing, about. The page presents a lot of information but doesn't quickly provide answers recruiters are looking for.

onboarding

Our area of research focused on how to improve the experience of talent acquisition managers so that reach the onboarding step, but as Erik walked us through his train of thought going through the onboarding process, we came up with 3 main suggestions:




Who is this for?

remove all individual-oriented questions like “dream industry” from onboarding



How can I make our company look great?

make asset sizing similar to more ubiquitous sites like twitter



What information do I need to give?

only ask for email and allow recruiters to see a preview of the process




CONCLUSION

We presented our findings to Christina and Kyle, two designers from Creative Guild. They were particularly interested in the "orientation" phase that could help direct different types of users to relevant content.

While our research covered pain points that prevented users from even reaching the onboarding process, a more comprehensive revision of the onboarding process to clearly address both creative individuals and recruiters would be a clear next step in ensuring users follow through with their interest in Creative Guild.
